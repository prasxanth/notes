Coin Oracle Method
How to Cast
×
Preparation: Still the mind. Hold your question clearly — not as a wish, but as a genuine inquiry into the forces at play.
The Method
Three coins are thrown six times. Each throw produces one line of the hexagram, built from the bottom up. Heads = 3 (yang), Tails = 2 (yin). Sum the three coins:

6 = Old Yin ●○ (moving, becomes yang)
7 = Young Yang ⚊ (stable yang)
8 = Young Yin ⚋ (stable yin)
9 = Old Yang ○● (moving, becomes yin)

Moving lines are where the oracle speaks most directly. They show transformation — where you are in transition. The second hexagram formed by reversing moving lines shows where things are heading.
Reading: The hexagram is a mirror, not a verdict. Read the Judgment as the overall situation; the Image as the pattern of nature to emulate; the moving lines as specific counsel for the moment.
